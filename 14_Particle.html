Particle

Particle is a small localized object to which can be ascribed several physical properties, such as volume and mass. Particle can be visualized as a ball having some mass, physical dimensions and occupying some volume. Unlike waves , particles are localized in space.

In physics, a particle is a fundamental unit of matter or energy. It is a term used to describe the smallest, indivisible entities that make up the physical world. Particles can be either elementary particles or composite particles.

Elementary particles are the basic building blocks of matter and are not composed of smaller particles. They are considered fundamental because they do not have any known substructure. Elementary particles include electrons and neutrinos, photons, quarks, and gauge bosons.

Composite particles are made up of combinations of elementary particles. They are formed when elementary particles bind together through various forces. Examples of composite particles include protons and neutrons, which are composed of quarks bound together by the strong nuclear force. Composite particles can have properties and behaviors that differ from their constituent particles.

Physicists distinguish particles of great variety in size: subatomic particles (10-18-10-16 m) like the electron, microscopic particles (10-10-10-8 m) like atoms, and macroscopic particles (10-6-10-4 m) like powders.

Particles can have mass, electric charge and other properties that determine their behavior and interactions with other particles. They can also exhibit wave-particle duality, meaning they can exhibit both particle-like and wave-like characteristics under certain conditions.
