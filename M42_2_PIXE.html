Particle induced X-ray emission (PIXE)

Overview

Particle-induced X-ray emission or proton-induced X-ray emission (PIXE) is a non-destructive analytical technique used to determine the elemental content of a substance from the X-ray emission caused by the irradiation of the substance with high-energy charged particles (such as protons or ions). The elements are identified according to the X-ray energy lines that are characteristic of each element. The X-ray intensities at specific energies are used to quantify each element in the sample under analysis.

Research tasks & applications

PIXE is a powerful analytical technique that can provide valuable information about the elemental composition of a sample. technique, called microPIXE using tightly focused beams (down to 1 μm) an be used to determine the distribution of trace elements in a wide range of samples. PIXE is used in a variety of applications, including environmental analysis, materials science, and cultural heritage preservation helping answer questions of provenance, dating and authenticity.

Principle

Particle-Induced X-ray Emission (PIXE) is an analytical technique that involves bombarding a sample with high-energy charged particles, such as protons or ions. The principle behind PIXE is that when these particles interact with the atoms in the sample, they can cause the ejection of inner-shell electrons. This creates vacancies that are subsequently filled by outer-shell electrons, resulting in the emission of characteristic X-rays that can be detected and analyzed to determine the elemental composition of the sample.
