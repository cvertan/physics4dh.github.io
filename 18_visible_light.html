Visible light

Visible light is part of electromagnetic spectrum perceived by the human eye. On the electromagnetic spectrum, the visible light spectrum falls between infrared and ultraviolet waves and has wavelengths between 380 and 750 nm. This range is just a tiny part of the entire electromagnetic spectrum, so our eyes can see just a little fraction of all the electromagnetic radiation around us.

Stars, candles, light bulbs emit visible light. It makes things able to be seen, so that we get visible information about our word. This makes visible light of the great importance for our life.

Image source: https://cdn.britannica.com

The spectrum of visible light contains waves of all rainbow colors: violet, indigo, blue, green, yellow, orange, red. Each color has a different wavelength. Red has the longest wavelength, and violet (∼ 700 nm) has the shortest wavelength (∼ 400 nm).

We can see the colors of the rainbow separately, when for example, the Sun shines on tiny droplets of moisture. When all the color waves are seen together, they make white light.

Beside the sight, there are other important uses for visible light. For example, lasers emitting in the visible range are widely used in surgery, CD players and laser pointers. Visible light waves also make our TV, computer and cell phone screens work
